World Health Organization

Delegates in the WHO committee will research the driving causes of the two committee topics. This will allowed for informed discourse during the conference and the creation of realistic, practical solutions. An emphasis will be placed upon delevolpment of a wide-ranging understanding of the problems, considering environmental, geopolotical economic and social factors, among others. Overall, delegates should expect to develop a deep understanding of both topics for a successful committee, including understanding past successes and failures in addressing these issues. Finally, delegates should expect to discuss the commitments that the country they represent can make, and what steps they can reasonably take to target the problems in their own country.

My name is Reuel Warner-Rosen, and I will be leading the WHO committee for WAMUNC 2018. I have participated in Model UN since my freshman year of high school, and this is my second year working with WAMUNC. Model UN has had a great impact upon me and my co-chairs, and we will do all that we can to ensure that this committee is equally impactful for you. Model UN has great potential to shape your future, teaching you critical research, writing and speaking skills that will benefit you for the rest of your life. However, in order to reap the rewards of Model UN, one must actively engage with the research, writing, and speaking.

The amount of work that you do in preparation for the conference will directly affect how much you get out of it, as well as how well you do in the conference. In addition to reading this background guide, you must do significant background research to represent your countryâ€™s interests in this committee. To create effective solutions, one must have a strong grasp of the history of the problem, as well as of past attempts to solve the problem. In conducting research for this committee use a diverse set of resources. The quality of your research will have the greatest impact on how well you do, and how much you take away from this experience.

When drafting solutions, be sure to establish how they will be funded, what NGOs will be involved, where they will be implemented, and any other necessary specifics. The more detailed and realistic your solutions are, the better they will work. The solutions that you create will be the basis for discussion in committee, so be sure to make them detailed, creative and effective. In committee, please be sure to listen to your fellow delegate and work together to draft comprehensive solutions. Model UN is all about working in a team. I look forward to meeting all of you, and if you have any questions, feel free to email me at ruliwr@gwu.edu.
